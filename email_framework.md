# Procedure Topic: Recruitment  
## Standardized Recruitment Email Communication Framework

---

## Problem Statement

Currently, recruitment communication across HAAG projects is informal and varies by team. Offer emails, rejection emails, and follow-up communications are written ad hoc by individual recruitment leads or admins. This creates several operational issues:

- Inconsistent tone and professionalism  
- Unclear deadlines or required actions  
- Ambiguity around next steps  
- Increased administrative time spent drafting repetitive messages  
- Potential reputational risk due to inconsistent communication standards  

While recruitment decisions themselves may be effective, the communication layer lacks procedural standardization. This initiative aims to improve clarity, transparency, and efficiency through a standardized recruitment email framework.

---

## Initiative Goal

The goal of this initiative is to design and evaluate a standardized recruitment communication procedure that includes reusable email templates for use across HAAG projects.

The deliverables will include:

- A documented recruitment communication procedure  
- A template bank including:
  - Offer email
  - Rejection email
  - Waitlist email
  - Offer deadline reminder  

---

# Initiative Framework

## 1. Discovery

To understand the current state of recruitment communication, I will:
- Interview 2–3 recruitment leads or admins about how they currently send recruitment decisions  
- Review examples of past offer and rejection emails   

This discovery phase will establish a baseline understanding of how recruitment communication currently functions and where ambiguity or inefficiencies may exist.

---

## 2. Hypothesis

My hypothesis is:

If HAAG adopts standardized recruitment email templates with clear structure, deadlines, and defined next steps, then:

- Recruitment communication will become more professional and consistent  
- Administrative drafting time will be reduced
  
---

## 3. Experiment – Cognitive Walkthrough & Heuristic Evaluation

Because the semester’s hiring cycle has concluded, a live pilot is not feasible. Instead, this initiative will use an expert-based evaluation method combining a cognitive walkthrough and heuristic evaluation to assess the effectiveness of the standardized recruitment email templates.

### Procedure

1. Develop standardized templates for:
   - Offer email  
   - Rejection email  
   - Waitlist email  
   - Offer reminder  

2. Conduct a cognitive walkthrough for each template by simulating the perspective of a first-time applicant.

For each email, I will evaluate:
- Is the purpose of the email immediately clear?
- Does the applicant understand what decision has been made?
- Is the required action obvious?
- Is any deadline clearly stated and unambiguous?
- Are next steps transparent?
- Could any wording reasonably cause confusion?

3. Conduct a heuristic evaluation of each template using established usability principles, including:

- **Clarity and Visibility of Status** – Does the email clearly communicate the applicant’s status?
- **Match Between System and Real-World Language** – Is the language simple and free of unnecessary jargon?
- **Consistency and Standards** – Is structure consistent across templates?
- **Error Prevention** – Are deadlines, actions, and contact information clearly stated to prevent miscommunication?
- **Minimalism and Relevance** – Is unnecessary information removed to reduce cognitive load?

4. Document any violations of these principles and revise templates accordingly.

---

## 4. Metrics for Evaluation

The evaluation will focus on:

- Reduction of ambiguity in required actions  
- Clear communication of applicant status  
- Presence of explicit deadlines and next steps  
- Structural consistency across templates  
- Adherence to usability heuristics  

If the templates demonstrate clear status communication, defined next steps, and no major heuristic violations, the procedure will be considered effective.

---

## 5. Report & Iterate

Following the cognitive walkthrough and heuristic evaluation:
- Identified ambiguity or usability violations will be documented  
- Templates will be revised to address issues  
- A finalized recruitment communication procedure will be produced  

The final artifact of this initiative will include:
- A standardized recruitment email template bank  
- A documented evaluation summary  
- An implementation guide for future semesters
