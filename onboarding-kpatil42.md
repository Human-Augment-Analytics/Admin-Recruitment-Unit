# Topic Area: Recruitment — Vector DB Project (HAAG)

**Kiran Patil**  
**kpatil42@gatech.edu**

This initiative focuses on standardizing how the Vector DB Project recruits, selects, and onboards contributors each semester. The goal is to consolidate key documentation (project scope, operations, expectations, tooling) and create a reusable recruitment template—potentially embedded as a set of Slack channel modules/pinned posts—so new members can ramp up quickly and project leadership can consistently evaluate and onboard candidates. This will reduce onboarding friction, preserve institutional knowledge, and ensure continuity across semesters.
---

## 1) Brainstorm 5 procedures that could be observed within HAAG related to Recruitment

1. Maintain a centralized **Recruitment + Onboarding Hub** (one doc with scope, expectations, links, onboarding checklist).
2. Define and publish standardized candidate qualification criteria (skills, availability, coursework, interests, security/compliance awareness if applicable).
3. Run a structured recruitment cycle (announce → collect applications → shortlisting → interviews/async screening → selection).
4. Use a consistent evaluation rubric for applications and interviews (technical fit, reliability, communication, motivation, availability).
5. Execute an onboarding workflow (tool access, repo setup, required readings, starter tasks, mentorship assignment, first-week milestones).

---

## 2) How can you collect information in HAAG to form a procedure for this operation?

- Interview Dr. Mussman and current project leads about recruitment goals, capacity constraints, and pain points from prior semesters.
- Interview experienced contributors about what helped/hurt onboarding (access issues, unclear expectations, missing docs, tooling friction).
- Review prior semester handoff notes, onboarding docs, and contribution patterns (who ramped fastest and why).
- Extract recruitment/onboarding signals from historical Slack threads (questions that repeat, common blockers, clarifications).
- Review GitHub repo activity (issues/PR flow) to identify which onboarding steps correlate with early productivity.

---

## 3) What resources would you need to consult to collect this information?

- Vector DB Project documentation (current README, architecture notes, backlog, roadmap, environments, tooling setup guides).
- Slack channel history (recruitment posts, onboarding questions, recurring issues, office hour notes).
- Prior semester reports / handoff documents / retrospectives.
- Direct input from Dr. Mussman, project leads, and senior contributors/mentors.
- Any existing HAAG-wide onboarding templates or guidelines that can be reused.

---

## 4) How will you validate that your procedure is correct?

- Review the drafted procedure with Dr. Mussman and at least one senior contributor for accuracy and completeness.
- Pilot the procedure with a small set of new recruits and gather feedback after Week 1 and Week 3.
- Compare outcomes before vs. after implementation:
  - time-to-first-PR / first meaningful task completion
  - number of repeated onboarding questions in Slack
  - reduction in access/setup blockers
  - self-reported clarity of expectations and confidence

---

## 5) What need is your procedure serving?

- Reduces ambiguity and improves consistency in candidate selection and onboarding.
- Speeds up ramp-up time and improves early productivity.
- Improves candidate–project fit (right skills and availability for the project’s needs).
- Preserves institutional knowledge across semesters and reduces dependency on tribal knowledge.
- Creates a scalable recruitment template other HAAG projects can repl.icate.

---
